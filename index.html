  • The "Search Bypass" ‚Äî Funnel theory assumes SEO/SEM capture awareness, yet all personas start from marketplaces or social platforms.
  • The "Convenience-Control Tension" ‚Äî Online shopping is convenient, but even frequent shoppers still want to touch and try products physically.
  • The "Data Awareness Gap" ‚Äî Consumers acknowledge data risks but continue shopping due to resignation and normalization.

Conclusion

This analysis reveals that contemporary Turkish e-commerce consumers‚Äîparticularly Gen Z and time-constrained professionals‚Äîoperate within a socially-mediated trust ecosystem that diverges significantly from traditional funnel assumptions. The interviews demonstrate that:

  • Community validation has replaced brand authority as the primary trust mechanism.
  • Platform ecosystems (Trendyol, Amazon) have displaced search engines as discovery tools.
  • Post-purchase experience quality determines loyalty more than product satisfaction alone.
  • Security awareness exists without behavioral change ‚Äî consumers acknowledge risks but continue shopping.

E-commerce strategies must evolve from transaction-focused funnels to relationship-focused ecosystems that prioritize social proof, seamless returns, and community engagement over traditional marketing and technical security displays.
